Reference & Rules Guide

If you are undertaking a major renovation, the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) provides tax relief to help offset construction costs. The project must meet strict legal definitions.

1. The 90% "Substantial Renovation" Threshold

Your project must pass the CRA's 90% test to be treated as a newly constructed home.

  • The Rule: At least 90% of the interior must be physically removed or replaced.
  • What Counts: Livable square footage (main floors, finished basements/attics).
  • What Doesn't Count: Structural elements (foundation, roof, exterior walls) and unfinished spaces.

2. The Appurtenance Rule (Detached Garages)

If the 90% test is met, the entire residential complex qualifies.

  • The Rule: The rebate covers the house, land, and appurtenances.
  • Detached Garages: Qualify as an appurtenance if built concurrently with the renovation for personal use.

3. The Primary Residence Requirement

Must be for use as the primary place of residence for you or an immediate relation. No secondary/vacation properties.

4. Rebate Limits and 2026 Enhanced Rules

  • Standard Rules: Federal GST rebate (max $6,300) phases out at $450k FMV. Ontario Provincial rebate (max $24,000) has no FMV cap.
  • 2026 Enhancements: For projects starting between April 1, 2026, and March 31, 2027, the combined rebate can reach up to $130,000 for homes under $1.5M.
